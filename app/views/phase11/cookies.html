Cookies

The Manage Your Authorised Testing Facility (MyATF) service puts small files (known as 'cookies') onto your computer to collect information about how you browse the site.

Cookies are used to:

  • measure how you use the website so it can be updated and improved based on your needs
  • remember the notifications you've seen so that we don't show them to you again
  • direct you down certain journeys to improve your experience
MyATF service cookies are not used to identify you personally.

You’ll normally see a message on the site before we store a cookie on your computer.

Find out more about how to manage cookies.

How cookies are used on the MyATF service

There are a number of different types of cookie used in the MOT testing service which allow the system to work securely and consistently. These are:

  • Google Analytics cookies
  • MOT testing service application cookies
  • SmartSurvey feedback cookies
  • Imperva Incapsula cookies
  • HotJar cookies

Google Analytics cookies
We use Google Analytics software to collect information about how you use MOT testing service. We do this to help make sure the site is meeting the needs of its users and to help us make improvements, for example improving how you enter test results.

Google Analytics stores information about:

  • the pages you visit on the MOT testing service
  • how long you spend on each page
  • how you got to the site
  • what you click on while you're visiting the site

Google does not collect or store your personal information, for example your name or address, so this information can't be used to identify who you are. We don't allow Google to use or share our analytics data.

Session management and security

The MyATF service uses cookies for session management and security. No personal identification data will be stored in any browsers. Once the browser is closed those cookies will be removed.

Name Purpose Expires

Imperva Incapsula cookies

Imperva Incapsula cookies help the Imperva Incapsula service identify malicious activity.

The cookies collect information in an anonymous form. They don't collect or store any personal information (eg names or addresses) so this information can’t easily be used to identify who the user is.

The only constant cookie is the Visitor ID (visid_incap_{ID}). Other Imperva Incapsula cookies are removed within 15 minutes or once the browser is closed.

Name Purpose Expires
incap_ses_{ID} Contains an encrypted Session ID. Used to relate HTTP requests to a particular visit. When you close your browser
nlbi_{ID} A network load balancer cookie used by the Incapsula application delivery platform. When you close your browser
visid_incap_{ID} Visitor-Cookie containing Visitor ID. This cookie is used to relate sessions to a specific visitor's device. One Year
